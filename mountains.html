About The Mournes

The Mournes are Northern Ireland's highest mountain range boasting a wide variety of walks.
Made up of 12 different mountains the highest being Slieve Donard at 850m.
Within the Mountains, you have the Mourne Wall which covers 22 miles along the Mountain range.
The Mournes off such a vast range of walks and hikes to suit every person.

Slieve Donard

Slieve Donard is the tallest of the mountains within the Mournes at 850m.
Donard offers some incredible views of the Irish sea and the landscape inland. On a clear day at the summit, you can see Belfast 30 miles to the North and Dublin 55 miles to the south
At the foot of the mountain you have the town of Newcastle which offers to park for the mountain,
it also offers some great restaurants and pubs for after your hike.

Time: 4-5 hours

Slieve Commedagh

Slieve Commedagh is the second-highest mountain at 767m, it is also the second-highest mountain in Northern Ireland.
At the Peak of Slieve Commedagh, the views of the other mountains and the Irish sea are Phenomenal.
The walk-up Commedagh is quite strenuous so be prepared.
You can get to the foot of Slieve Commedagh from Newcastle.

Time: 4 hours

Slieve Binnian

Slieve Binnian is 746m high and is Northern Ireland's third-highest mountain.
Binnian offers a challenging hike but boasts some incredible views and landmarks on the way to the peak. One of these landmarks is at the midpoint of the hike where you can stop and take in the view of the silent valley and the surrounding Mournes.
At the summit, the views across the landscape are breathtaking.
The foot of mountain has car parking and Carrick Cottage Cafe is a great place to grab some food before a long hike!

Time: 3-4 Hours

Slieve Bearnagh

Slieve Bearnagh is 739m high, it has a distinct look with two tors with a gap between them.
You can find Bearnagh positioned to the southwest of Newcastle.
The Area around Slieve Bearnagh is popular for runners, camping, and hiking.
Slieve Bearnagh is a very difficult and tiring climb, experience is key
You'll be able to find parking at Trassy Park.

Time: 4-5 hours

Slieve Lamagan

Slieve Lamagan sits at 704m high. Lamagan is the hardest of the Mournes due to the fact it is positioned in the middle.
Due to this, you have to cross two peaks to reach Lamagans peak which makes the walk harder but so muchmore rewarding.
Once reaching the foot of Lamagan the climb is a steep walk to the peak.
This hike is recommended for experienced hikers.
You can start this hike at Carrick Little.

Time: 4-5 Hours

Slieve Meelbeg

Standing at 702m Slieve Meelbeg is located in the west of the Mournes.
Meelbeg is the twin of Meelmore due to it being next to each other and you can walk both in half a day.
Meelbeg is the taller of the two.
Due to the nature of having Meelbeg and Meelmore so close to each other,
it is highly recommended to do both of them in a walk offering an extra challenge to the walk.
You can begin your walk from the Trassy car park.

Time: 3-4 hours if covering both Mountains

Slieve Meelmore

Slieve Meelmore sits at a height of 680m, it is twinned with Slieve Meelbeg which is to the east of Meelmore.
Meelmore is the shorter of the two peaks.
Due to Meelmore being so close to Meelbeg you can visit these both in one walk.
Car parking can be found at Trassy car park.

Time: 3-4 hours if covering both mountains
