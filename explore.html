The Mourne Wall

The Mourne wall is one of the most distinct parts of the Mournes covering a staggering 22 miles and crossing 15 of the highest peaks.

Once entering the area it is hard to not see the astonishing length of the wall among the stunning view of the mountains

Built between 1904 and 1922 it is built of granite from the local area, it was built to protect the watercourse from cattle.

Exploring different parts of the wall will bring you to so many beautiful & breathtaking parts of the Mournes which include Silent valley.

If you are up for a challenge you can do the mourne wall challenge that encompasses 7 out of the 10 highest peaks, it takes on average 14-15 hours to complete and is not for the light-hearted.

Silent Valley

Silent Valley Reservoir is surrounded by some of the beautiful Mourne mountains.

The reservoir has been a major source of water for Belfast in 1933 since it was built.

Silent Valley mountain park offers an amazing way to explore this part of the Mournes.

With so many magical trails to walk with picturesque views of the landscape, this is a must-day out for anyone visiting the Mournes

Within the mountain park, they have picnic areas, a playpark, and a cafe as well which make this an ideal place for families to have a great day out!

Mountain Biking

Mountain biking in the Mournes offers some amazing opportunities to explore the area in a very different way. Within the Kingdom of Mourne, you have numerous places to visit to go mountain biking.

Three of the most popular places are Rostrevor, Castlewellan, and Tollymore, and all offer different trails for all skill levels.

You don't have to stick to the trails, if you fancy something more relaxing go to silent valley for an evening bike ride or got off course and discover your own routes.

There are lots of active groups for you to join and make some new friends!

Rostrevor


Rostrevors trails offer a variety of difficulty from beginner to seasoned pro.
Being located next to Carlingford Lough these trails offer some stunning views over the Lough.
You can find parking located at Kilbroney Park.


Trails


  • Red Trail - 27km - 3 Hours
  • Black Trail - 19km - 2 Hours
  • Mega Mission - 1.8km Downhill
  • On The Pulse - 1.3km Downhill

Facilities


  • Cafe
  • Bike Hire
  • Parking
  • Toilets

Castlewellan


Castlewellan is a great day out for the whole family wanting to experience mountain biking but also adds the thrill for the most experienced.
Being based around Nothern Ireland's most famous lake boasts a stunning castle beside it.


Trails


  • Green Trail - 4km - 30 Minutes
  • Blue Trail - 4.5km - 45 Minutes
  • Red Trail - 19km - 2 Hours
  • Dolly's Chute - Black Option
  • The Great Escaps - Black Option

Facilities


  • Cafe
  • Bike Hire
  • Parking
  • Toilets

Tollymore


Tollymore is a single trail based in the magical Tollymore forest Park.
The center that is based here offers courses for novices and intermediates to either get you started or improve your skills
The trail is open to anyone to use so if you want to improve taking berms this is the place to go! To find out more about the center and the courses click here.
