The Mourne Wall

The Mourne wall is one of the most distinct parts of the Mournes covering a staggering 22 miles and crossing 15 of the highest peaks.

Once entering the area it is hard to not see the astonishing length of the wall amoungst the stunning view of the mountains

Built between 1904 and 1922 it is built of granite from the local area, it was built to protect the water course from cattle.

Exploring differnt parts of the wall will bring you to so many beautiful & breathtaking parts of the Mournes which incluse Silent valley.

If you are up for challange you can do the mourne wall challange that encompasses 7 out of the 10 highest peaks, it takes on average 14-15 hours to complete and is not for the light hearted.

Mountain Biking

Mountain biking in the Mournes offers some amazing opertunties to explore the area in a very differnt way. Within the the Kingdom of Mourne you have numerious places to visit to go mountain biking.

Three of the most popular places are Rostrevor, Castlewellan and Tollymore and all offer different trails for all skill levels.

You dont have to stick to the trails, if you fancy something more relaxing go to silent valley for an evening bike ride or got of course and discover your own routes.

There are lots of active groups for you to join up and make some new friends!

Rostrevor


Rostrevors trails offer a veriety of difficulty from beginner to seasoned pro.
Being located next to Carlingford Lough these trails offer some stunning views over the Lough.
You can find parking located at Kilbroney Park.


Trails


  • Red Trail - 27km - 3 Hours
  • Black Trail - 19km - 2 Hours
  • Mega Mission - 1.8km Downhill
  • On The Pulse - 1.3km Downhill

Castlewellan

Being based around Nothern Irelands most famous lake boasts a stunning castle beside it.
